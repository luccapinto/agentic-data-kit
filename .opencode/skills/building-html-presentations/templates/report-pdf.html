Executive summary

Bookings beat plan by 12%, but growth is concentrated in enterprise and increasingly at risk in SMB

Q1 bookings reached $4.2M (+12% vs. plan, +35% over four quarters). The upside is real but narrow: two regions and the enterprise segment carry most of it, while SMB and discounting are early warning signs for H2.

Growth is steady but accelerating in enterprise

Bookings rose every quarter; the Q4→Q1 step was the largest, led by enterprise deals.

2

Concentration risk

The top two regions account for ~70% of bookings, leaving the result exposed to a few accounts.

3

SMB is softening

SMB attainment fell to 73% as deals stalled late-stage; win rate dipped quarter-over-quarter.

4

Margin pressure from discounting

Competitive deals closed at higher discounts, diluting realized price on ~1 in 5 deals.

Key takeaways

  • Double down on the enterprise motion that drove the beat.
  • De-risk concentration by broadening the mid-market pipeline.
  • Intervene on SMB qualification and discount governance now.
  • Re-forecast H2 with explicit downside scenarios.

So what — and next

Four moves to protect the upside and de-risk the quarter

Scale the enterprise motion

Reallocate capacity to the segment that beat plan; formalize the winning play. Owner · timeline.

Broaden mid-market pipeline

Reduce concentration risk by building coverage beyond the top two regions. Measure: pipeline by region.

Fix the SMB funnel
